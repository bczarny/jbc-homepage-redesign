The JBC difference

Not just navigating the future — shaping it.

Our premise

At JBC, we recognize that media relations is evolving rapidly, reflecting the changing ways in which audiences consume information. As traditional news cycles shift towards a more dynamic landscape, we're committed to adapting our strategies to meet these new challenges and opportunities.

Our innovative approach elevates us beyond mere information dissemination; we are storytellers at the forefront of brand visibility and community connection. By harnessing strategic storytelling tailored to diverse platforms, we ensure that each message resonates deeply with target audiences while aligning seamlessly with our clients' core values and objectives.

01 · Point of view

Storytelling at the speed of culture.

Traditional news cycles have fractured. We build programs that move with the platforms audiences actually live on — not the ones the press release was written for.

02 · Posture

Consumer affinity over noise.

Visibility is easy. Affinity is compounding. Every placement, partnership, and piece of content ladders to a measurable lift in how the brand is understood.

03 · Practice

Aligned with your values.

Brand objectives get you a strategy. Brand values get you the right strategy. We scope to both from kickoff — and the work reflects it.

What makes us different

Four things you won't find elsewhere.

Most PR agencies sell a service mix. JBC sells a worldview — a specific take on who deserves the loudest microphone, how we earn the right to wield it, and what to do with the news cycle once we have it.

01 · Focus

Built for founder-led brands.

The best stories start with the founder. JBC was built to translate founder expertise — the point of view, the conviction, the category insight — into narratives that move category perception. We're specialized to do that work, not to fit a founder story into a template built for a corporate brief.

Our bench, our book, and our reporter relationships are weighted toward moving founder-led consumer brands from overlooked to essential. We partner with executives and category leaders who are actually building something — and we show up to pitch the story, not the product.

02 · Lens

DEI is not a chapter.

Everything we do is viewed through a DEI lens — from pitch strategy to spokesperson selection to the outlets we put on the A-list for every brand. That isn't a quarterly add-on; it's a structural filter that runs through the way we staff accounts, prep executives, and measure whose stories get amplified.

We back it up internally with strategic hires and an ongoing monthly educational series that keeps the full team sharpened on evolving best practices across race, gender, class, and ability. The goal: a team that doesn't need to be asked to do the work — it's already in the scope.

03 · Speed

Newsjacking & trend forecasting.

JBC has its pulse on the news cycle. We don't just react to what's happening — we decode, synthesize, and predict. Our analytical reporting turns the chaos of a news day into a clear map of where a brand's voice belongs, where it doesn't, and which trend curves are worth betting on 48 hours before the rest of the category notices.

That same rigor runs as a forward-looking service: quarterly trend briefs, monthly category scans, and real-time Slack-speed flag-ups when a cultural moment opens a window to insert the brand meaningfully. Speed without strategy is noise. Strategy without speed is a press release. We run both.

04 · Generosity

Office Hours — free, monthly.

Once a month, JBC opens the doors. Office Hours is a free session for founders and early-stage brand leaders to ask the PR questions they can't ask anywhere else — how to think about a launch, how to prep for a first profile, when to escalate a crisis, and what the agency pitch-deck promises actually mean in practice.

We do it because the right advice at the right moment shapes a category. Book a slot at officehours@jbc-pr.com, or subscribe to the Office Hours newsletter on Substack to keep up with the weekly read on where the news cycle is headed.

We're not just navigating the future of media relations — we're shaping it.

The companion Office Hours Substack is where we publish our read on the week — the stories the team is tracking, the cultural cues we think are about to break, and the unglamorous tactical notes on what it actually looks like to ship a program inside a news cycle that never stops moving.

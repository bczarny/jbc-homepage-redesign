01 · Voice & tone

Editorial. Confident. Direct.

JBC writes the way it pitches — with a point of view, in plain language, without the industry bloat. The voice is an agency of humans talking to humans, not a deck talking to a committee.

Principles

Confident, not cocky.

JBC has a real point of view — the writing reflects that. Short declaratives, periods on headlines, no hedging. "Bold thinking. Bigger results." not "Maybe you're looking for..."

Editorial, not salesy.

Read the copy aloud. If it sounds like a sales page, cut it. JBC is a PR firm — we write the way editors write. Specific verbs, real metrics, no superlatives without proof.

Human, not corporate.

"We" and "you." Contractions are fine. Em-dashes are encouraged. One real sentence beats three hedged ones.

The italic-serif accent

One of the most distinctive JBC brand moves: most headlines contain a single word in italic serif (Fraunces) in accent red. It carries the emphasis. Never more than one per headline, never a full phrase.

Use it like this

Bold thinking. Bigger results.

One word — the key word — carries the italic serif treatment.

And like this

What we're writing about.

Works for verbs, nouns, modifiers — whatever gets the most weight.

Don't do this

Bold thinking. Bigger results.

Italicizing everything erases the emphasis.

Or this

Bold thinking. Bigger results.

Full phrases in italic become decoration, not accent.

Copy conventions
Do

Sentence case headlines with a period. "Not a bigger agency. A better one."

Don't

Title Case Every Word. Or drop the period entirely.

Do

Specific verbs: "repositioned," "built," "earned," "translated."

Don't

Vague verbs: "leveraged," "utilized," "drove synergies."

Do

Mono ALL-CAPS eyebrows for metadata only (category, date, section label).

Don't

ALL-CAPS for headlines or body copy. It reads like shouting.

Do

Proof points with real numbers: "20B+ impressions," "52% of attributable revenue."

Don't

Unsupported superlatives: "industry-leading," "best-in-class," "world-class."

02 · Logo

Two wordmarks. One mark.

JBC has a single wordmark rendered in two versions — dark for cream backgrounds, cream for dark backgrounds. Never apply effects, outlines, or color treatments beyond these two.

Dark wordmark · jbc-logo-trimmed.png

Use on cream (`#FFF6EB`), pale, or any light background. This is the default lockup for nav, mobile menu, favicons.

Cream wordmark · jbc-logo-white.webp

Use on ink (`#0A0A0A`) and dark photography. Standard for the footer.

Clearspace & minimum size

Leave clearspace equal to the height of the "b" in "jbc" around all sides of the wordmark. Minimum size: 48px wide in digital applications, 0.75" in print. Don't reproduce below those.

What not to do
  • Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the wordmark.
  • Don't add drop shadows, strokes, gradients, or color.
  • Don't place over low-contrast photography — use a solid cream or ink band instead.
  • Don't recreate the wordmark in another typeface.
03 · Color

Cream, ink, and the accent.

The palette is warm and restrained. Cream and ink carry 90% of the surface area; accent red is reserved for the italic-serif word, the bullet, and hover states. Two neutrals fill in the mid-tones.

Core palette

Ink --ink #0A0A0A · RGB 10, 10, 10 Primary text, primary button, dark section fills (scorecard, WWD, footer).
Accent --accent #D64A1F · RGB 214, 74, 31 Italic serif words, hover states, bullet dots, category tags on light.
Accent ink --accent-ink #8A2F14 · RGB 138, 47, 20 Inline links, italic serif emphasis in body copy, deeper accent variant.
Neutral & supporting tones
Cream 2 --cream-2 #F6E8D2 Warmer band / section divider (Why JBC, Offices).
Pale --pale #FBF0DE Card fill (case study, Spin teaser, principles).
Ink 2 --ink-2 #2B2B2B Body copy. Softer than pure ink for long reads.
Muted --muted #6A6258 Eyebrows, mono metadata, captions.
Line --line rgba(10,10,10,0.12) Hairlines, card borders, dividers.
Line strong --line-strong rgba(10,10,10,0.22) Ghost button borders, breadcrumb separators.
Usage rules
  • Accent is earned. Reserved for one italic-serif word per headline, the bullet dot on eyebrows, and red hover states.
  • Never use accent for body copy. For inline italic emphasis in body copy, use accent-ink (`#8A2F14`).
  • Large dark sections (ink) alternate with cream sections to create rhythm — scorecard and What We Do live on dark; everything else is cream.
  • Pale and cream 2 are subtle band variants — use to separate sections within a cream stretch without going dark.
04 · Typography

Three families. One voice.

Helvetica Neue carries the display and body. Fraunces shows up only as the italic-serif accent. IBM Plex Mono handles every eyebrow, caption, stat label, and metadata line.

Families
Display & Body
Helvetica Neue

Weights 400 (regular) and 500 (medium). Tight letter-spacing on display sizes (-0.035em to -0.045em). Fallback stack: "Helvetica Neue", Inter, Arial, sans-serif.

Italic serif accent
Fraunces

Weight 400 italic. Used ONLY for accent words, blockquote emphasis, and attribution. Never set a full paragraph in Fraunces. Fallback: EB Garamond, Georgia, serif.

Metadata & eyebrows
IBM Plex Mono

All-caps with 0.14–0.22em tracking for eyebrows, stat labels, category tags, breadcrumbs, and copyright lines. Never use mono for headlines or body.

05 · Grid & spacing

Fluid gutters. Anchored container.

A single max-width container (1320px for most pages, 1280px for this guide) with fluid gutters that scale from 20px on mobile to 56px on desktop. Vertical rhythm comes from clamp(), not fixed breakpoint values.

Container

Max-width: 1320px · gutter: clamp(20px, 4vw, 56px)

Every page centers content in a 1320px container with fluid horizontal gutters. Sections breathe with vertical padding between clamp(64px, 9vw, 140px).

Vertical rhythm

Section padding: clamp(64px, 9vw, 140px)

Each major section gets generous vertical breathing room that scales with viewport. Section heads carry their own internal padding via the `.section-head` grid.

Radii

Small 6px · Medium 14px · Large 22px

Cards use medium (14px) by default. Ghost buttons and pills use 999px (full round). Inputs use small (6px). Feature cards use large (22px) sparingly.

06 · Components

The kit.

Every button, eyebrow, badge, card, and tile across the site. Use these as-is for web; adapt the proportions for decks and print without changing the logic.

Buttons

Pill buttons — ink primary, ghost outline.

Buttons are always pill-shaped (border-radius: 999px). Primary is ink-on-cream; ghost is outlined on cream. Both shift to accent red on hover. .btn.lg modifier for hero CTAs.

Buttons · dark

Inverted for dark sections.

On ink backgrounds (scorecard, footer CTA block) the primary button flips: cream fill, ink label. Ghost becomes cream outline on ink.

Eyebrow

Mono label with accent bullet.

Every section header and card lede starts with an eyebrow: mono, uppercase, 0.18em letter-spacing, with a 6px orange bullet dot at the start. Use for category, status, or the section's thesis in 2–3 words.

07 · Imagery

Editorial. On-record.

Photography reads like editorial — real clients, real press, real moments. The cream palette shows through in tones and retouching: warm, low-saturation, a hint of print.

Team / portrait · warm tones

Portrait · warm tones.

Leadership and team photography uses natural light, cream/beige tones, and a tight portrait crop. No studio gradients or stock backgrounds.

Product / client hero

Client hero · product-forward.

Case study tiles use the brand's own product or campaign imagery. Kept at saturation 1.05 and contrast 1.02 — enough to pop, not enough to fight the cream surface.

Press / editorial spread

Press spread · editorial quote.

When showing coverage, use the outlet's real typesetting. Italicize outlet names in body copy (Fraunces italic) — "in Forbes," "across Fast Company."

Office / environment

Office · natural light.

Environment photography (the press office, the NY and LA spaces) leans cream and neutral. Landscape crop for in-line use; 10:9 for intro blocks.

09 · Motion

Restrained. Deliberate.

Motion is always in service of content — never decoration. Four patterns cover 90% of the site: hover lifts, arrow slides, scale-on-hover for images, and intersection reveals.

Hover · lift
Card lift on hover

Cards translate up 3–4px with a .4s cubic-bezier(.2,.8,.2,1) easing. Subtle — just enough to signal interactivity.

Hover · arrow
Arrow slide

CTAs end with →. On hover the arrow translates right 3–6px over .2s ease. Applied to every "Read case study," "Read the piece," "See all work."

Hover · image
Image scale-in

Case study and Spin images scale from 1 → 1.03 over .6s cubic-bezier. Container has overflow: hidden so the image crops on zoom.

Scroll · reveal
Intersection reveal

Elements with class .reveal fade from opacity 0 / translateY(18px) to 0/0 over .7s ease when they enter the viewport. Reduced to none under prefers-reduced-motion.

Nav · scrolled
Sticky nav shadow

The nav picks up a subtle bottom border + cream backdrop-blur once scrolled past 10px. Keeps the wordmark legible over any content below.

Mobile · menu
Full-screen overlay

Mobile menu slides down from translateY(-100%) over .35s ease. Close button sits inside the overlay. Body gets overflow: hidden while open.

10 · Applications

How to apply this.

For work that isn't a web page — capabilities decks, proposals, one-pagers, keynote slides — pull from this system in the proportions below.

Capabilities decks & proposals.

  • Surface: Cream (`#FFF6EB`) as the default background. Alternate two or three dark (`#0A0A0A`) spreads for section breaks — scorecard, services, exec-speaking moments.
  • Headlines: Helvetica Neue 500, tight letter-spacing. Always finish with a period. Use one italic-serif Fraunces accent word per slide headline.
  • Eyebrows: Mono caps, tracked 0.18em, with the orange bullet. Use on every section divider slide.
  • Stats & numbers: Big display numbers (40–80pt), 0.7em italic-serif accent for the "+" or qualifier, mono label underneath.
  • Quotes: Italic-serif opening mark in accent red, sentence-case blockquote, mono attribution.
  • Case studies: One per spread — image left, body right. Eyebrow + client name + lede + 2–3 stat pairs.

One-pagers & client briefs.

  • Cover: JBC cream wordmark on ink band at top or bottom. Client logo centered. Project title in display with one italic-serif accent.
  • Structure: Eyebrow · headline · lede · 3–4 supporting sections. Mono-caps section labels separate content.
  • Margins: Generous — 0.75" minimum on all sides. Let the page breathe; don't fill it.
  • Signature: "Jennifer Bett Communications · NY & LA · info@jbc-pr.com" in mono caps at the footer.

Email signatures.

  • Line 1: Full name (display, ink, medium).
  • Line 2: Title (mono caps, muted).
  • Line 3: "Jennifer Bett Communications" in display.
  • Line 4: City (NY or LA) · direct email · jenniferbett.com — all mono caps.
  • No: No animated gifs. No vCards. No hot pink. No 12-line disclaimers.

Social & IG templates.

  • Press spotlights: Cream background, brand product or portrait centered, outlet wordmark at top in serif, pull-quote in sentence case.
  • Trend forecasts: Title in display with italic-serif accent, month + year in mono caps, supporting lede in body.
  • Reels: Open with the eyebrow bullet card, close with the JBC wordmark. Keep mono cap text for context, display for emphasis.
  • Observer / awards posts: Keep the tan/beige tone. Observer wordmark in Fraunces italic. Names in sentence-case display underneath.
